Chair history


The school of Probability theory at St.Petersburg State University was founded by the great Russian mathematicians P.L. Chebyshev, A.M. Lyapunov and A.A. Markov whose works determined the development of Probability Theory for many years. Their predecessor V.Ya. Buniakovsky, published the first Russian textbook on Probability (Gauss used this book for learning Russian language); in particular, in this textbook he considered original problems of statistical quality control.

The probabilistic works of Chebyshev and his school were later continued by S.N. Bernstein.

A new era of this research direction started in 1948 by foundation of the Chair of Probability Theory and Mathematical Statistics as a unit of the Department of Mathematics and Mechanics. Its founder, Yury Vladimirovich Linnik, was already famous at that time for his outstanding discoveries in Nuber Theory and started working also in Probability. A large group of actively working students formed soon, attracted by his impressive personality.

In 50-th Yu.V. Linnik, N.A. Sapogov, O.V. Sarmanov, V.P. Skitovich, V.V. Petrov, I.A. Ibragimov, were among Chair's faculty. In 60-th the Chair was joined by Yu.A. Davydov, Ya.Yu. Nikitin, L.V. Osipov, A.G. Bart and later by V.B. Nevzorov, S.S. Vallander, V.V. Nekrutkin, S.M. Ermakov and V.N. Solntsev (in 1979 the latter trio and A.G.Bart left for the new Chair of Statistical Modelling founded by S.M.Ermakov on the base of our Chair's section). S.M. Ananievskii, A.I. Martikainen, A.N. Frolov, O.V. Rusakov, M.A. Lifshits, V.L. Abbakoumov, A.N. Tikhomirov became the Chair's faculty later.

N.N. Vorobiev, V.N. Sudakov, A.M. Kagan, M.S. Nikouline, V.N. Solev, M.I. Gordin, V.A. Egorov, A.N. Borodin, A.Yu. Zaitsev, L.V. Rozovsky, S.V. Malov, E.B. Ponikarov, D.N. Zaporozhets, N.V. Gribkova collaborated by teaching Chair's courses while holding their principal positions elsewhere. Most of them earlier graduated from our Chair.

After Yu.V. Linnik, the Chair was headed by V.V. Petrov (in 1960-1995), I.A. Ibragimov (in 1995-2005) and Ya.Yu. Nikitin (since 2005).

The main directions of scientific work at the Chair are
  • limit theorems of Probability Theory
  • theory of random processes
  • asymptotic methods of mathematical statistics
  • charachterisation problems
The corresponding important research results published in Russian and foreign journals and monographs are widely acknowledged and obtained important AWARDS.

The Chair's faculty is teaching Probability and Statistics not only within mathematical department but also in other departments of the university. The most important connections the Chair maintains with the deprtment of Economy. The first lectures in Probability at this department were given by professors A.A. Markov (the son of the founder of Markov processes' theory) and by Yu.V. Linnik himself.

Many hundreds of students graduated from the Chair after having passed specialization in probability and statistics. In sixties and seventies from 20 to 50 memoirs were being defended annually at the Chair. The current annual number of defences is close to 15.

The Chair's graduates are successfully working in Russia and abroad. Mor than 40 of them obtained the degree of Doctor of Science (the second and supreme scientific degree in Soviet Union and Russia). Then 1961-graduate Chen Hanfu became a member of Chinese Academy of Sciences. Former Ph.D.-students of Yu.V. Linnik and V.V. Petrov, W. Wolf, G. Christoph (currently the professor of Magdeburg University), and T. Mikosch (currently the professor of Copenhagen University), became the leading specialists of Germany (GDR) in Probability Theory.

The current Chair's faculty team contains 10 Doctors of Science.

Chair's faculty lectured, as invited professors, at the universities of USA, France, Germany, Italy, Canada, Australia, Belgium, Finland, Mexico and other countries.

It would be fair to claim that Chair of Probability and Statistics is one of the most successful chairs of St.-Petersburg University.

V.V. Petrov
