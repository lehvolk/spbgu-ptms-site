AWARDS


A number of scientific works produced at the Chair of Probability Theory and Mathematical Statistics was distinguished by prestigious prizes.

Yu.V. Linnik and I.A. Ibragimov were awarded by Lenin Prize (the highest scientific honor of the USSR) and elected to the Soviet (now Russian) Academy of Sciences as full members.

The Markov Prize was awarded to V.V. Petrov (1971) by the Soviet Academy of Sciences and A.Yu. Zaitsev (2009) by the Russian Academy of Sciences.

The Prizes of St.Petersburg (formerly Leningrad) State University for the best scientific works were awarded in different years to Yu.V. Linnik, N.A. Sapogov, V.A. Statuleviçius (at that time a Ph.D. student of the chair; later he became the head of a vast Lithuanian school of probability theory), V.V. Petrov, S.M. Ermakov, L.V. Osipov, Ya.Yu. Nikitin, V.B. Nevzorov and M.A. Lifshits.

The Saint-Petersburg (formerly Leningrad) Mathematical Society awarded its annual prizes to Yu.A. Davydov, M.A. Lifshits, V.V. Vysotsky, R.S. Pusev.

M.I. Gordin was awarded by honorary title "Gaussian Professor" by Gottingen Academy of Sciences (2013).
